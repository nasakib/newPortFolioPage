Intro to Me:



My interest in software development and computation began in 5th grade. I discovered that I could modify the free version of Halo: Combat Evolved to give me features found in the full version. I was fascinated by the community of game developers and modifiers that I encountered in my endeavors. The things they taught me seemed almost magical, and I knew that I wanted to learn how it all came together.

I was given my first cell phone in my freshman year of highschool, the Motorolla Moto X 1st Gen. I broke the screen within 4 months of ownership, and I discovered yet another internet community that taught me how to fix it. Those skills were the catalyst that started my side hustle in highschool: fixing phone screens, and replacing the batteries while I was at it. Soon I found myself as a go-to for phone repairs amongst friends and family. I then built my first computer using the money earned. After helping a couple of friends with their own build, I began planning and assembling builds for other people as well.

Basically, what I want to emphasize is that I've always taken initiative to aquire skills and knowledge in relevant fields of interest.

Fast forward to today, I'm a student learning to become a full stack software developer at the Georgia Institue of Technology. The field of software development and computation is vital to the modern age, and I'm looking forward to making an impact with the skills that I'm curating.
